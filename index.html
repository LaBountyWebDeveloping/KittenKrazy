Star's Fun Facts

Star was the first kitten in the house to begin jumping up on the kitchen counters. After he started, he soon got all of the other kitties to jump up there as well. He was the second to learn manners. After he lets out a big kitten sneeze, his humans bless him and he replies with either a "thanks" or an "excuse me". His humans obviously have no way of telling what his meows mean, they just know he's doing something unusual for a feline.

Casper's Fun Facts

Somehow the sweetest yet meanest. He is known as Evie's number one bully. Casper was the second kitten to be found napping under the bathroom sink.

Lion's Fun Facts

Lion was the first kitten to learn manners; in fact, he was born with them. When his humans first picked him up to bring him home, he was the first to begin making connections with them through long periods of eye contact. It was so prominent on that day that his humans continue to say that his wide, loving eyes make him look like an adorable owl.

Noel's Fun Facts

Noel was the first of the bunch to be given a middle name, which she was given because she looks like a kangaroo. She was also the first to singe a few of her belly hairs on a Montana Magic Candle. Her humans didn't think she knew what was going on, but luckily they were right there to save her and show her that fire is "OUCH!". Perhaps from burning her hair, or perhaps just because she's Noel, she developed a love of wax. Her humans make homemade candles and Noel loves to join them as they scoop out wax to melt. She's even tried running off with some before to play with. She is the first kitty to sleep in sinks regularly.

Eve's Fun Facts

She doesn't like to speak of it, but Evie once did happen to have an accident in the bedroom one night. She forgot to go before bedtime, something her humans have somehow trained a from-stray-to-indoor-kitten to know how to do. She was the first kitten to be Photoshopped. Eve was also the first to be a bathroom kitten in quarentine. She has no problem walking across her human's necks at night and can usually be found hogging one of their pillows.

Bean's Fun Facts

Even though he is the youngest and smallest baby kitten in the house, he is the heaviest and widest. All his humans care about, though, is his health. It's actually better for him to have a few extra ounces in case of his URI flaring up. He takes burrying leftover food very seriously and is willing to knock things over and move them across the room just to slightly cover his meal.
